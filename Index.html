🎯 Phase 02 · Define
The 4 W's & Problem Statement
Narrowing the discovery findings into a crisp, actionable problem space.
Problem Statement
Urban professionals on tiffin subscriptions cannot customize their daily meals, leading to menu fatigue, unnecessary food waste, and high churn — because kitchen operators rely on fixed bulk-cooking to stay profitable.
W
What
Professionals cannot customize daily subscription meals, leading to dissatisfaction and food waste.
W
Why
Traditional tiffin models rely on cooking one massive batch of a fixed menu to maintain low costs.
W
Where
Urban environments — working professionals in offices or bachelor accommodations in metro cities.
W
How
Users receive unwanted dishes 2–3×/week, cannot swap them, and lose money if they cancel the day.
💡 Phase 03 · Develop
Ideation & The Core Concept
How do we give users freedom without destroying the kitchen's economics? The "Base + Swap" credit model.
🔄
Base + Swap System
The core innovation
The kitchen still cooks in bulk — but prepares 3 specific menu variations instead of one. Users get a default meal but can swap individual items using in-app credits before 8:00 PM the night before.
Example Swap
🍆 Eggplant Curry
→
🧀 Paneer Masala
Why this protects the kitchen
1
Kitchen cooks only 3 variations — not 50. Bulk efficiency preserved.
2
Swaps must be requested by 8 PM the night before — giving prep time.
3
Credits limit excessive swapping while still giving users real autonomy.
Key Features Identified
⏭️
Skip a Day
Rolls daily cost into next month's credit. Zero money lost.
📅
Visual Calendar
Full week's menu visible at a glance. Plan ahead with confidence.
🔄
Swap Interface
One-tap bottom sheet to replace dishes before the nightly cutoff.
🧪
Taste Profile Quiz
Onboarding sequence that blacklists hated ingredients permanently.

User Personas
Who We're Designing For
Two distinct archetypes that define every design decision in FlexiTiff.
👨‍💻
Rohan Mehta
28 · Software Engineer · Metro City
"I work 10-hour days. I want a home-cooked meal when I get back, but I refuse to eat the same soggy eggplant and dal three days a week."
Goals
Balanced, high-protein diet — no cooking
Easy pause for travel or dinner plans
Reduce personal food waste
Pain Points
WhatsApp-only service communication
Can't swap rice for extra protein
Loses money on skipped prepaid days
Design Opportunity
Visual Meal Calendar with 1-tap "Skip & Roll Over Credit" + easy component swap before 8 PM.
👩‍🎨
Priya Sharma
23 · Junior Architect · New City
"I miss my mom's cooking. Restaurant food is too expensive, and my current tiffin is incredibly bland."
Goals
Affordable, comforting home-style meals
Variety to beat menu fatigue
Tight monthly budget management
Pain Points
Stuck with karela every fixed Tuesday
Must cancel whole month for one bad meal
No way to flag disliked ingredients
Design Opportunity
Onboarding "Taste Profile" with ingredient blacklist — auto-suggest alternatives when banned items appear on the menu.
